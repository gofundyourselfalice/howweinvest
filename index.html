Introduction

How do you invest?

In 2026, I asked the @gofundyourself community a simple question: how do you invest?

Not how should you invest. Not what the textbooks say. Not what a financial advisor would recommend. Just: what do you actually do with your money?

Over 500 people replied, leaving more than 1,400 individual answers across 10 open-ended questions and 7 polls. Goals, mistakes, platforms, monthly amounts, what to avoid, what they wish they'd known, and what investing has actually done for their lives.

This guide is the result. Every number, chart, and quote comes directly from those responses.

What this guide is (and isn't)

Investing — and by investing I mean building your wealth over the long term — has historically been the preserve of finance experts and men in suits. This is changing, but there are still lots of people in the UK who could be making the most of this opportunity. Perhaps one of them is you.

This guide is a window into how normal people (not finance bros, not City workers, not people with trust funds) actually invest. Their words, their numbers, their experiences. It is not a recommendation to do anything in particular. It's your money, your call.

A note on affiliate links

After creating this guide, I reached out to the top 10 providers mentioned asking if they would like to provide a sign-up offer and affiliate link. Some platforms mentioned in this guide have affiliate programmes. Where a platform has been linked, GFY may receive a small commission if you sign up. This has no influence on the data: the guide reports what the community said, not what anyone is promoting.

Important disclaimer. This guide is not financial advice. Nothing here is a recommendation to buy, sell, or invest in anything specific. It is a snapshot of what real people said they do with their money, based on anonymous Instagram Story responses collected in March 2026. Every person's financial situation is different. If you want personalised advice, speak to a qualified financial advisor. Investing can help your money work harder, but it comes with risk. The value of your investments can fall as well as rise, and you may get back less than you put in. Only invest what you can afford to leave alone.

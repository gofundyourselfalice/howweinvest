Common questions, answered

A few of the most common questions, answered from the guide and the community data. The full forum is on its way.

What is a stocks and shares ISA?
A tax-free wrapper for investments. You can pay in up to £20,000 a year (2025/26) and pay no UK tax on any growth or income inside it. It's how most of our community invests.
How much do I need to start investing?
Less than most people think. Many in our community started with £25–£50 a month, and being consistent matters more than the amount. 40% of respondents wish they'd started sooner.
What's the difference between active and passive funds?
Active funds have a manager picking investments to try to beat the market, usually at a higher fee. Passive funds simply track a whole market index automatically, which is much cheaper. Most of our community invests passively.
Which platform do most people use?
Among 500+ respondents, Trading 212 (about 31%) and Vanguard (about 23%) are the most-used, followed by Hargreaves Lansdown, Moneybox and Nutmeg. See Where you invest.
What should I do when the market drops?
Historically the hardest and best thing is usually nothing. Every major drop has eventually recovered, and panic-selling locks in losses. Not advice, and capital is always at risk.
At what age do people start investing?
The average starting age in our community is 31, with a range of 17 to 55.

How these platforms got involved

Once we'd analysed the data, we pulled together a top 10 list of the platforms our community uses most. Only then did we contact them, sending each one the same note, offering them the chance to add a sign-up offer and a comment of their own. Here's the email, more or less, that we sent:

A
Alice <alice@gfy.co.uk>
to  [Your platform] team
You're one of the most-used platforms in our investing project

Hi team,

I'm Alice, founder of Go Fund Yourself (GFY), an online community focused on financial transparency, with a monthly reach of 2 million predominantly UK-based women (@gofundyourself on Instagram).

We're publishing a crowdsourced investing guide based on polling our community on how UK women are actually investing in 2026. [Your platform] is one of the top platforms our community uses. There have been thousands of responses, and it includes details such as most popular funds and most common investing mistakes.

Would you like to add a sign-up offer for our readers and a short comment of your own?

Best,
Alice

We named each platform's own ranking in their version. Saying yes got them a sign-up offer and the space below to add their own words; saying nothing changed nothing about where they appear.

A note on higher-risk products: a couple of the platforms here also offer CFDs, high-risk leveraged products where the majority of retail accounts lose money. Nothing in this guide covers or recommends CFD trading. Check each provider's own current risk disclosures before opening an account.

On the cost figures: these estimate each provider's standard stocks & shares ISA account/platform charge, drawn from their own pricing pages and comparison sites like Boring Money (checked June 2026). Fees change often and fund/ETF charges are extra, so always confirm the current cost on the provider's site before opening an account.

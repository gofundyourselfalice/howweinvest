Glossary

Every term used in this guide, explained in plain English.

Capital gains tax (CGT)
Tax paid on profit when you sell an investment that has grown in value. Investments inside an ISA are exempt.
Compounding
When your investment returns generate their own returns over time. The longer your money is invested, the more powerful this effect becomes.
Dividend
A payment made to shareholders from a company's profits. Received as cash with an Inc fund; automatically reinvested with an Acc fund.
Diversification
Spreading your money across many different investments so that poor performance in one area doesn't sink your whole portfolio.
ESG
Environmental, Social, and Governance. A framework used to filter out companies that don't meet certain ethical standards.
ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
A type of fund that trades on the stock market like a share, so it can be bought and sold at live prices during the day. Most ETFs are passive and low-cost.
FSCS protection
The Financial Services Compensation Scheme protects up to £85,000 of your money per regulated firm if that firm fails.
Fund
A pooled investment that buys a collection of stocks, bonds, or other assets on behalf of investors.
Index fund
A type of passive fund that tracks a market index (e.g. the FTSE 100 or S&P 500) automatically, without a fund manager picking stocks.
ISA (Individual Savings Account)
A tax-free wrapper for savings and investments. The annual allowance is £20,000 (2025/26).
LISA (Lifetime ISA)
A type of ISA that offers a 25% government bonus (up to £1,000/year) for first-time buyers and retirement saving. You must be aged 18–39 to open one.
Platform
The service or app you use to buy and hold investments (e.g. Trading 212, Vanguard, Hargreaves Lansdown).
Portfolio
The total collection of investments you hold.
Pound cost averaging
Investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of market conditions, so you buy more when prices are low and less when they're high.
SIPP (Self-Invested Personal Pension)
A pension you manage yourself, with tax relief on contributions. You can't access the money until age 57 (rising to 58 by 2028).
Stocks and shares ISA
A type of ISA that holds investments rather than cash. No capital gains tax or dividend tax on anything inside it.
Stock / share
A unit of ownership in a single company.

Affiliate links. After creating this guide, the GFY team reached out to the top 10 providers mentioned asking if they would like to provide a sign-up offer and affiliate link. Some platforms have affiliate programmes. Where a platform has been linked, GFY may receive a small commission if you sign up. This has no influence on the data.

Not financial advice. Nothing on this page is a recommendation to buy, sell, or invest in anything specific. It is a snapshot of what real people said they do with their money. Investing can help your money work harder, but it comes with risk. The value of your investments can fall as well as rise, and you may get back less than you put in. Only invest what you can afford to leave alone.
